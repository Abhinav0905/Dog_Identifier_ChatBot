## Common Health Issues in Street Dogs

### Mange
Mange is a skin disease caused by mites and is extremely common in street dogs in India.

**Signs:**
- Hair loss, often starting on the face, ears, and elbows.
- Thickened, crusty, or greyish skin.
- Intense scratching and skin irritation.
- Sores from scratching.

**Severity:** Mange can be treated effectively, but requires veterinary intervention. Untreated mange causes severe suffering and can be fatal. It is transmissible between dogs and can cause temporary skin irritation in humans (though human mange from dog mites typically resolves without treatment).

### Distemper
A serious viral disease affecting the respiratory, gastrointestinal, and nervous systems.

**Signs:** nasal and eye discharge, coughing, lethargy, loss of appetite, vomiting, diarrhoea, and in advanced stages, seizures or behavioural changes. Vaccination prevents distemper.

### Parvovirus
Highly contagious and often fatal in unvaccinated dogs, particularly puppies.

**Signs:** severe vomiting, bloody diarrhoea, lethargy, and loss of appetite. Any puppy showing these signs requires emergency veterinary care.

### Tick-Borne Diseases
Common in the Himachal Pradesh region. Ticks transmit diseases such as Ehrlichiosis and Babesiosis.

**Signs:** fever, lethargy, loss of appetite, pale gums, and nosebleeds. Tick removal and veterinary treatment are required.

### Injuries from Road Accidents
Road trauma is one of the most common reasons street dogs are brought to DAR.

**Signs:** obvious wounds, inability to walk, shock (pale gums, rapid breathing, cold extremities). Requires immediate emergency care.

## Nutrition and Hydration

A healthy dog should drink approximately **50 ml of water per kg of body weight per day** (so a 10 kg dog needs around 500 ml daily). Dehydration is common in street dogs, especially in summer.

Signs of dehydration:
- Skin that doesn't spring back when gently pinched (skin turgor test).
- Dry, sticky gums.
- Sunken eyes.
- Lethargy.

If you are feeding a stray dog, providing fresh, clean water is as important as food.

## Summer Safety for Dogs in Dharamsala

Summer in the Kangra Valley can bring high temperatures that put street dogs at risk.

**Heatstroke prevention:**
- Ensure dogs have access to shade and fresh water.
- Avoid handling or moving dogs during the hottest part of the day (noon to 3pm).
- Paw pads can burn on hot pavement — dogs trapped on hot surfaces may be unable to walk away.

**Signs of heatstroke:** excessive panting, drooling, bright red gums, vomiting, staggering, and collapse.

**Response:** move to shade, apply cool water, contact a vet immediately.

## Anxiety and Fearful Behaviour

Street dogs that have experienced trauma, abuse, or lack of early socialisation may exhibit fearful or anxious behaviour.

**Signs:** excessive barking, cowering, hiding, trembling, destructive behaviour, or aggression born from fear.

**Do not approach a visibly terrified dog** — fear aggression is unpredictable and bites are common. Allow the dog to retreat. If the dog needs rescue, contact DAR who have experience handling fearful animals safely.

## Caring for Elderly or Disabled Dogs

Older dogs or those with permanent disabilities (from injury or illness) need:
- Softer sleeping surfaces.
- Help getting up on slippery or hard floors.
- Modified exercise — shorter, gentler activity.
- More frequent veterinary check-ups.
- Patience with slower movement and possible cognitive changes.

DAR accepts surrendered elderly and disabled dogs and works to find appropriate foster or permanent homes for them.
