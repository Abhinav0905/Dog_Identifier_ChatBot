# Dog Bite Safety and First Aid
Source: https://dharamsalaanimalrescue.org/when-street-dogs-attack/

## What to Do If a Street Dog Attacks

Street dog attacks are a serious concern in India. When a dog attack or rabies outbreak occurs, the delicate balance of dog/human coexistence is put under strain. Rather than taking matters into your own hands, always seek professional help.

### Immediate Steps After a Dog Bite

1. **Move to safety immediately** — put distance between yourself and the dog. Do not run, as this may trigger further chasing instinct. Back away slowly.
2. **Wash the wound thoroughly** — clean the bite wound with soap and running water for a minimum of 10 minutes. This is the single most important first aid step and significantly reduces infection risk.
3. **Apply antiseptic** — if available, apply an antiseptic such as povidone-iodine or alcohol to the cleaned wound.
4. **Cover the wound** — use a clean bandage or cloth to cover the bite.
5. **Seek medical attention promptly** — visit a doctor or hospital as soon as possible. A healthcare professional will assess the risk of rabies infection and advise on post-exposure prophylaxis (PEP).
6. **Report the incident** — inform local animal control or an animal rescue organisation so the dog can be monitored and vaccinated if needed.
7. **Note the dog's appearance and last known location** — this information helps rescue teams locate the animal.

### Do Not

- Do not attempt to handle, chase, or corner the dog after a bite.
- Do not delay seeking medical treatment — rabies is fatal if not treated promptly before symptoms appear.
- Do not attempt to kill or harm the dog — this is illegal in India and counterproductive to public health efforts.

### Rabies Risk

India accounts for one third of the world's estimated 59,000 annual rabies deaths. 99% of human rabies deaths result from dog bites. 40% of victims are children. This makes prompt wound care and medical attention critical after any dog bite, regardless of whether the dog appeared healthy.

Any mammal can carry rabies. Symptoms in humans typically appear 1–3 months after exposure, but the virus is 100% fatal once symptoms begin. Treatment before symptoms (PEP) is highly effective.

### Approaching a Stray Dog Safely

- Approach slowly and calmly — avoid direct eye contact initially.
- Allow the dog to sniff you first before reaching out.
- Never approach a dog that is eating, sleeping, or with puppies.
- Avoid sudden movements or loud noises near unfamiliar dogs.
- If a dog shows aggression (raised hackles, growling, bared teeth), back away slowly without turning your back.
