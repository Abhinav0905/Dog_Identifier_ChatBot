## Physical Signs of Distress

### Emaciation and Malnutrition
- Ribs, hip bones, and spine prominently visible.
- Significant muscle wasting, particularly around the hindquarters and shoulders.
- Dull, patchy, or unkempt coat.
- Sunken eyes.
- Lethargy and reluctance to move.

Emaciation is a **high-priority** concern. A severely malnourished dog needs immediate rescue intervention.

### Mobility Problems
- Limping or favouring one limb.
- Reluctance to stand up, lie down, or climb.
- Stiffness after rest, especially in the morning.
- Obvious swelling around joints.

This may indicate arthritis, an injury, or nerve damage. A dog that is unable to walk or move properly requires urgent attention.

### Wounds and Skin Conditions
- Open wounds, lacerations, or abscesses.
- Signs of mange — patchy hair loss, thickened or crusty skin, intense scratching.
- Swelling, redness, or discharge from a wound.
- Maggot infestation (myiasis) — a medical emergency requiring immediate veterinary care.

### Eye and Ear Problems
- Discharge from the eyes (clear, yellow, or green).
- Cloudy or red eyes.
- Squinting or pawing at the eyes.
- Head shaking or scratching at the ears.
- Foul smell from the ears.

### Respiratory Signs
- Coughing persistently.
- Laboured or rapid breathing at rest.
- Wheezing or unusual breathing sounds.
- Blue or pale gums (emergency sign — seek help immediately).

### Digestive Signs
- Vomiting repeatedly (more than once or twice).
- Blood in vomit or stool.
- Distended or very tight abdomen.
- Straining to defecate with little result.

## Behavioural Signs of Distress

- Unusual aggression or extreme fearfulness — may indicate pain or neurological problems.
- Disorientation or confusion — circling, bumping into objects, seeming lost.
- Excessive vocalisation (whining, yelping) without obvious cause.
- Complete loss of interest in surroundings (very different from normal resting).
- Seeking isolation — a social dog withdrawing completely.

## Fever in Dogs

Normal dog body temperature is 38–39.2°C (100.5–102.5°F). Signs a dog may have a fever:
- Warm, dry nose (though a wet nose does not rule out fever).
- Lethargy and loss of appetite.
- Shivering despite warm weather.
- Warm ears and forehead to the touch.
- Vomiting.

A fever above 40°C (104°F) is a medical emergency.

## Assessing Severity Quickly

| Observation | Priority Level |
|---|---|
| Unconscious or not breathing | Critical — emergency |
| Severe bleeding, suspected fracture | Critical — emergency |
| Unable to stand, severe emaciation | High |
| Deep wounds, maggot infestation | High |
| Obvious limping, eye discharge | Moderate |
| Dull coat, mild lethargy | Low to moderate |
| Appears generally healthy | Low |

## What to Report to Dharamsala Animal Rescue

When reporting a dog in distress, try to include:
- Location (nearest landmark or street name).
- Description of the dog (colour, size, approximate age).
- What you observed — specific visible signs.
- Whether the dog can move and how it is behaving toward people.
- Any context (was it hit by a vehicle, appears to be a stray, has a collar, etc.).
