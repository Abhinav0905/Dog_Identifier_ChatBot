## Key Facts About Rabies in India

- India accounts for **one third of the world's 59,000 annual rabies deaths**.
- **99% of human rabies cases** result from the bite of an infected dog.
- **40% of victims are children**, often under the age of 15.
- 54% of people surveyed in urban slums in India did not know that rabies was a disease — highlighting a critical gap in public awareness.
- Rabies is a zoonotic disease — it passes from animals to humans through saliva (usually a bite).
- The virus is **100% fatal** once clinical symptoms appear. However, it is **100% preventable** with timely post-exposure prophylaxis (PEP).

## The Global Plan to Eliminate Rabies by 2030

The WHO, FAO, WOAH, and GARC have set a goal of **zero human deaths from dog-mediated rabies by 2030**. The strategy has three pillars:

1. **Vaccinate dogs** — mass dog vaccination is the most cost-effective way to interrupt transmission. Vaccinating 70% of a dog population can eliminate rabies circulation.
2. **Educate communities** — raise awareness so people seek post-bite care immediately.
3. **Ensure access to PEP** — make post-exposure prophylaxis available and affordable.

## Dharamsala Animal Rescue's Role

Dharamsala Animal Rescue (DAR) conducts mass dog vaccination campaigns across the Dharamsala region. In one campaign alone, DAR vaccinated 347 homeless dogs. The organisation runs community education programs to increase awareness of rabies transmission and prevention.

The community plays the central role in making Dharamsala rabies-free:
- Reporting dogs that appear sick or unvaccinated.
- Supporting vaccination camps.
- Educating neighbours and children about safe behaviour around dogs.
- Not interfering with or harming vaccinated dogs (many carry an ear notch as evidence of vaccination).

## What to Do After a Potential Rabies Exposure

1. Wash the bite wound with soap and running water for at least 10 minutes immediately.
2. Apply antiseptic if available.
3. Go to a doctor or hospital the same day — do not wait for symptoms.
4. Ask your doctor about Post-Exposure Prophylaxis (PEP) — a series of rabies vaccine doses given after exposure.
5. PEP is effective if started before symptoms appear and must not be delayed.
6. Report the incident so the animal can be located and monitored.

## Signs of Rabies in Dogs

- Sudden change in behaviour (friendly dog becoming aggressive, or vice versa).
- Excessive drooling or foaming at the mouth.
- Difficulty swallowing.
- Disorientation or staggering.
- Unprovoked aggression.
- Paralysis of the hindquarters in later stages.

A dog showing these signs should not be approached. Report it immediately to Dharamsala Animal Rescue.

## Vaccination

Dogs in the Dharamsala area that have been vaccinated by DAR are typically identified by an ear notch. If you see a dog with an ear notch, it has been through DAR's program and is part of the region's rabies control effort. Do not harm or remove vaccinated street dogs — their presence in a territory actually helps prevent un-vaccinated dogs from moving in.
