# Dog Population Management in Dharamsala
Source: https://dharamsalaanimalrescue.org/dog-population-management-plan-in-dharamsala

## ANIMAL BIRTH CONTROL (ABC) or Catch, Neuter, Vaccinate, Return (CNVR)

To stabilize population growth, 90% sterilization must occur within a defined area.* *Per scientists and researchers from organizations like the World Health Organization (WHO), Global Alliance for Rabies Control (GARC) and the International Companion Animal Management Coalition (ICAM).

The Method is not one size fits all but here are suggested components Mapping your area, Running population surveys, Have trained staff to run and catch and release sterilization program Defining impacts and measuring success

- Impact 1: Improve dog welfare
- Impact 2: Improve care provided to dogs
- Impact 3: Reduce dog density/stabilise dog population turnover
- Impact 4: Reduce risks to public health
- Impact 5: Improve public perception
- Impact 6: Improve rehoming/adoption centre performance
- Impact 7: Reduce negative impact of dogs on livestock
#### Step 1: Defining and Mapping the Area. Running Initial Population Survey

December 2017, DAR conducted its first population survey using a method based on suggestion by ICAM. Findings: In Dharamsala Town (Section 25) there are approximately 1000 stray roaming dogs. In the surrounding areas the numbers were inconclusive due the rural setting and varying altitude.

December 2018, DAR conducted a second survey focusing on the areas outside of Dharamsala town, using a capture – vaccinate – recapture plan based on the Lincoln Peterson Method. Hunter Dowds , a pre-med student from Lawrence University, received a grant to help us with this program, including vaccinating 347 dogs.

The map is the area that DAR is currently working on. The green areas were randomly selected (by our scientific consultants) as the first 6 areas to target. There is an estimated 4200 dogs in the entire mapped section and 706 in the 6 green areas per our survey result.

#### Step 2: Training the Team

Prior to 2022, our small team of 13, was not hitting our goals for many reasons; lack of trained vets, Covid, paravets coming and going. After years of trying, we received the great news that Worldwide Veterinary Service (WVS) would come to DAR for 3 weeks to train our vets, paravets, animal welfare assistants, and management on how to run a humane and effective animal birth control program. The results following:

2022 – 1000 sterilizations

2023 1007 sterilizations
