## About Dharamsala Animal Rescue (DAR)

Dharamsala Animal Rescue is a registered animal welfare organisation based in Dharamsala, Himachal Pradesh, India. The organisation's mission is to create "a world of difference for animals and people" by:

- Rescuing and treating injured and sick street animals.
- Running vaccination and sterilisation programmes (ABCAR).
- Facilitating adoption of animals that cannot survive independently.
- Educating communities on animal welfare and safety.
- Addressing the intersection of animal welfare and public health (particularly rabies).

DAR operates across the Dharamsala region, including McLeod Ganj, Dharamkot, Bhagsu, and the surrounding Kangra Valley.

## How to Report a Stray Dog in Distress

When you see a stray dog that appears to be injured, sick, or in danger, the most important thing you can do is **report it quickly with clear information**.

### What to Include in Your Report

1. **Exact location** — nearest landmark, street name, or GPS coordinates if available. Be as specific as possible; rescue teams need to find the animal quickly.
2. **Description of the dog** — approximate size, colour, distinguishing features (e.g., ear notch, collar, injury to specific leg).
3. **Observed condition** — what you saw that concerned you. Specific details help triage staff prioritise response:
   - Is the dog moving or immobile?
   - Are there visible wounds?
   - Is the dog conscious and responsive?
   - Any signs of bleeding?
4. **Behaviour** — is the dog aggressive, fearful, or unresponsive to people nearby?
5. **Context** — was it hit by a vehicle? Has it been there for hours/days? Are there other dogs with it?

### While You Wait for Rescue

- **Keep a safe distance** — do not try to handle or restrain the dog unless you are trained.
- **Do not attempt to move the animal** unless it is in immediate danger (e.g., on a busy road) and you can do so safely with a second person.
- **Stay nearby if it is safe to do so** — you can guide rescuers to the exact location.
- **Note any changes** in the dog's condition to report to the rescue team.
- **Keep other people and animals away** from the dog.

### Do Not

- Do not offer food or water to a dog with a suspected abdominal injury.
- Do not restrain an aggressive dog — report and wait for a trained responder.
- Do not leave without noting the exact location, even if you cannot stay.

## What Happens When DAR Responds

1. A rescue team is dispatched to the reported location.
2. The team assesses the dog's condition on arrival.
3. Emergency first aid is administered at the scene if needed.
4. The dog is transported to DAR's veterinary facility for examination and treatment.
5. Treatment records are maintained and the dog's progress tracked.
6. Once stable, dogs are assessed for return to the street, foster placement, or formal adoption.

## Adoption

DAR facilitates the adoption of street dogs that:
- Have been injured or are unable to survive safely on the street.
- Have been abandoned by owners.
- Are puppies requiring a home environment.

Both Indian and international adoption is supported. Visit dharamsalaanimalrescue.org/adoption/ for current available animals.
